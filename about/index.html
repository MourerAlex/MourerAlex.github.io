Background

I work at the intersection of causal inference, biostatistics, and machine learning. My interest is in what we can credibly learn about cause and effect from observational data — the messy, real-world kind that doesn't come from randomised trials.

This site is a place to share my thinking, work through ideas in public, and build a body of writing that I hope is useful to others learning these methods.

I'm also available for freelance work in data analysis, statistical consulting, and scientific writing. If you're working on a project where causal thinking matters, feel free to get in touch.
