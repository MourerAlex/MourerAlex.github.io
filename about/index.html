Making causal analysis a little more casual.

I'm a data scientist with roughly ten years of experience, four of them in pharma working on real-world evidence. I hold a PhD in statistics and machine learning, and my day-to-day sits at the intersection of causal inference, biostatistics and epidemiology.

This site is a working notebook — mostly for statisticians, epidemiologists, clinicians, and anyone nerdy about causal inference. Posts start as notes I write to myself to understand a paper or work through a derivation; the ones that feel useful get cleaned up and published. The aim is short, opinionated, and honest about what's assumed.
